How I Transformed Restaurant Ordering with a User-Centric Mobile App.

Noque. Mar 24.

Engineered an innovative mobile app simplifying restaurant interactions through streamlined ordering and efficient pickup.

Overview.

Restaurant apps are notoriously frustrating. Too many steps, too much friction between "I'm hungry" and "food is ready." Noque was designed around a single principle: every unnecessary tap removed is a moment of stress eliminated. The entire ordering flow fits in three decisions.

Approach.

The design started from the pickup moment — working backwards from the counter to the first screen. What does someone need to know right before they arrive? What do they need to decide? Everything else is noise. The result is an app that feels closer to a conversation than a form.

Details.

Ordering.
3 steps

Browse, select, confirm. No account required for first-time orders. Payment is handled with a single tap via Apple Pay or Google Pay.

Pickup.
Live status

A live progress indicator shows exactly when to head to the counter. No more awkward hovering or repeatedly asking staff if the order is ready.

History.
Reorder

Past orders are surfaced immediately on open. One tap to reorder your usual — because the best UX is the one you never have to think about.
