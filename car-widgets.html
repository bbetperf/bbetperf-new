How I Designed Apple-Inspired Car Widgets for a Safer and Smarter Drive.

Car Control Widgets. Oct 24.

Crafted native-style widgets for car controls, bringing Apple's clarity and consistency into everyday driving.

Overview.

Apple's iOS widget system set a new standard for glanceable, purposeful interfaces. This project explored what happens when you apply that same discipline to car controls — bringing familiar patterns into the driver's cockpit without sacrificing safety or ergonomics.

Approach.

Each widget was designed around a single, primary action. Climate. Navigation. Music. Phone. The goal was to make the most frequent interaction happen in one tap — and the less frequent ones available without hunting. Apple's SF Symbols library provided a shared visual grammar that drivers already trust.

Details.

Layout.
Grid

Widgets snap to a 4-column grid with consistent corner radii and padding — matching the spatial language of iOS home screen widgets for instant familiarity.

Iconography.
SF Symbols

SF Symbols scale cleanly at every size and carry existing mental models. Drivers don't need to learn new icons — they already know what they mean.

States.
Active / Idle

Active widgets glow with a tinted fill. Idle ones fade to neutral. The system communicates state through color alone, no text required.
