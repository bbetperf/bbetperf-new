Reading. Sep 25.

A short manifesto for what Readings is, how I use it, and how to read it.

What This Is.

Readings is not a blog. It's not a newsletter. It doesn't have a publication schedule or a theme. It's a collection of things I noticed — observations about design, interfaces, objects, and experiences that seemed worth writing down in more than a tweet but less than an essay.

Most of what goes here is triggered by using something well, or using something badly. A navigation prompt that makes you anxious. A watch that respects your attention. A button label that tells you what it does instead of what it is. Small things that reveal larger thinking.

How to Read It.

Each piece is short by design. If it takes more than three minutes to read, it should have been edited more. The goal is a single idea, clearly stated, with enough context to be useful to someone who builds interfaces for a living — or anyone who pays attention to the ones they use.

Nothing here is definitive. These are observations, not arguments. If something reads like a strong opinion, it probably is one — but opinions about design are always provisional. The next product that does it differently and better is already being designed somewhere.
