Why I'm Starting Readings.

Hello, World! Sep 25.

A short manifesto for what Readings is, how I use it, and how to read it.

I'm launching Readings as a place to write about things I notice—in design, in products, in the world. Not essays, not tutorials. Just observations that feel worth writing down.

The Reason.

I think a lot about products and systems, and most of those thoughts disappear. I wanted a place to make them last a little longer—somewhere between a notebook and a portfolio piece. More developed than a note, more honest than a case study.

The name "Readings" came from thinking about how you read a room, read a situation, read someone's intentions. It's not about the act of reading books. It's about interpretation—looking at something and trying to understand what it's doing and why.

What Will Be Here?

Short pieces about design decisions I find interesting. Things like:

— A navigation app that reduces driver stress through word choice, not features.

— A watch that died and came back because people loved what it stood for.

— Interface patterns that do more than they say.

These aren't meant to be comprehensive analyses. They're meant to be starting points—observations that open something up rather than close it down.

What to Expect Next?

I don't have a posting schedule. I'll write when something seems worth writing about. The plan is to keep pieces short enough to finish in one sitting and specific enough to be about something real, not just a vague topic.

Takeaway.

Readings is a way for me to think in public without performing. If something here is useful to you, great. If it just stays a personal record of what I found interesting in a given month, that's fine too.
